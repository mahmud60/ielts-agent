# IELTS Listening Test – Section 1

**Topic: Renting an Apartment**
**Speakers: Sarah (Tenant) and Mr. Collins (Letting Agent)**

---

*[Phone ringing]*

**Mr. Collins:** Good morning, Sunrise Property Agency. This is Mr. Collins speaking. How can I help you?

**Sarah:** Oh, hello. My name is Sarah Bennett. I'm calling because I'm looking to rent an apartment in the city centre. I saw your listing online.

**Mr. Collins:** Great, Sarah. You've called the right place. We have several properties available. Can I take a few details first?

**Sarah:** Of course, go ahead.

**Mr. Collins:** So, are you looking for a furnished or unfurnished apartment?

**Sarah:** Furnished, please. I've just moved to the city and I don't have any furniture yet.

**Mr. Collins:** Understood. And how many bedrooms are you looking for?

**Sarah:** Ideally two bedrooms. One for myself and one for a home office.

**Mr. Collins:** Perfect. And do you have a budget in mind?

**Sarah:** I'd like to keep it under nine hundred pounds per month if possible.

**Mr. Collins:** That's very reasonable. We actually have a lovely two-bedroom apartment on Hartfield Road. It's fully furnished and costs eight hundred and fifty pounds per month.

**Sarah:** Oh, that sounds promising. Which floor is it on?

**Mr. Collins:** It's on the third floor, and there is a lift available in the building.

**Sarah:** Wonderful. Does it have a parking space?

**Mr. Collins:** Yes, it includes one parking space in the underground car park. There's also a storage unit in the basement if you need extra space.

**Sarah:** That's very useful. What about bills? Are any included in the rent?

**Mr. Collins:** Water is included, but electricity and gas are separate. Internet is also not included, but the building has a fibre connection ready to activate.

**Sarah:** I see. And how long is the minimum rental period?

**Mr. Collins:** The minimum contract is six months, but most tenants sign for twelve months.

**Sarah:** I'd be happy with twelve months. Can you tell me about the deposit?

**Mr. Collins:** The deposit is equivalent to six weeks' rent, which would be approximately one thousand two hundred pounds. It's held in a government-approved scheme.

**Sarah:** That's fine. Is the apartment near any public transport?

**Mr. Collins:** Yes, there's a bus stop right outside and Greenfield tube station is just a five-minute walk away.

**Sarah:** Excellent. What about shops and supermarkets nearby?

**Mr. Collins:** There's a large supermarket about two minutes on foot, and the city centre shopping area is within walking distance.

**Sarah:** This sounds perfect. Is it available immediately?

**Mr. Collins:** It becomes available from the first of next month. We'd need your application form and references before then.

**Sarah:** Absolutely. Could I arrange a viewing this week?

**Mr. Collins:** Certainly. Are you free on Thursday afternoon, say around two o'clock?

**Sarah:** Thursday at two works perfectly for me.

**Mr. Collins:** Wonderful. I'll book that in. Can I take your phone number to confirm the appointment?

**Sarah:** Yes, it's 07745 312 896.

**Mr. Collins:** Let me read that back — 07745 312 896.

**Sarah:** That's correct.

**Mr. Collins:** Great, Sarah. We'll see you on Thursday. If you have any questions before then, don't hesitate to call us.

**Sarah:** Thank you so much, Mr. Collins. I'm looking forward to it.

**Mr. Collins:** Have a lovely day. Goodbye.

**Sarah:** Goodbye.

---

*[End of Section 1]*
